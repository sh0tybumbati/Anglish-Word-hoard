# New Albion Anglish Wordbook

## Computing & Technology

**Computer/Processing:**
- **Reckoner** - computer
- **Reckon gem** - processor/CPU
- **Thinkgear** - computer (alternative)
- **Runeblock** - data storage device (hard drive, memory)
- **Reckon ticks** - binary code/digital signals

**Programming & Code:**
- **Runewright** - software developer/programmer
- **Rune** - code/programming language
- **RuneScript** - program code

**Electrical Systems:**
- **Sparkcraft** - electricity
- **Sparkways** - electrical conduits/wires
- **Sparkcircles** - electrical circuits
- **Sparkkeepers** - batteries
- **Sparkstones** - capacitors

**Hardware Engineering:**
- **Thinkwright** - computer/hardware engineer
- **Thinkgear craftwright** - computer engineer (full form)

## Metals & Materials

**Raw Materials:**
- **Ironstone** - metal ore (generic)
- **Grey ironstone** - iron ore
- **Red ironstone** - copper ore
- **White ironstone** - silver ore
- **Yellow ironstone** - gold ore

**Refined Metals:**
- **Grey iron** - iron/steel
- **Red iron** - copper
- **Silver iron** - silver
- **Gold iron** - gold
- **Swift iron** - aluminum
- **Strongiron** - steel (high-strength)
- **Lightiron** - aluminum (alternative)
- **Softiron** - tin

**Alloys:**
- **Redsoft iron** - bronze (copper + tin)
- **Redwhite iron** - brass (copper + zinc)

**Specialized Categories:**
- **Sparkiron** - highly conductive metals for circuits

## Military & Equipment

**Armor:**
- **Wardplate** - powered armor/knightly power armor

**Weapons:**
- **Glowbrand** - power sword/energy sword

## Magnetism & Fields

**Magnetic Materials:**
- **Lodestone** - natural magnet/magnetic ore
- **Lodeblock** - refined magnet
- **Sparklodestone** - electromagnetic device

**Magnetic Fields:**
- **Lodefield** - magnetic field
- **Sparklodefield** - electromagnetic field
- **Ironreach** - magnetic field (alternative)

## General Terms

**Places:**
- **New Albion** - homeland/nation

**Descriptive Terms:**
- **Runewise** - digital/symbolic
- **Wrought** - artificially made
- **Aftermade** - artificial/imitation

## Etymology Notes
All terms constructed using Old English roots and Germanic compound word patterns to create "neoarchaic" vocabulary suitable for a fantasy/science fiction setting where magic and technology are ambiguous.
